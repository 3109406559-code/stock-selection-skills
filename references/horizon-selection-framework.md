# Horizon Selection Framework

Use this file to score candidates across the three holding horizons.

## Short Term

Target window:

- next trading day to roughly 5 trading days

Look for:

- strong concept or policy catalyst
- clear next-session tradability
- strong recent turnover and price action
- healthy support from the relevant sector or concept
- a clean invalidation level

Avoid:

- one-word limit-up names that are not realistically tradable
- illiquid names where exact entry prices are not believable
- `ST` or delisting-risk situations
- names where apparent strength depends only on stale data

## Medium Term

Target window:

- roughly 2 to 12 weeks

Look for:

- earnings momentum or estimate revision logic
- positive industry trend
- 20-60 day base, breakout, or relative strength
- catalysts expected over the next quarter
- reasonable risk/reward from the latest completed-session close

Avoid:

- stories driven only by a one-day rumor spike
- weak balance-sheet names without a clear catalyst path
- names with insufficient 20-60 session structure

## Long Term

Target window:

- roughly 6 to 24 months

Look for:

- durable business quality
- sound balance sheet and capital allocation
- reasonable valuation band
- supportive long-term industry trend
- a thesis that survives normal short-term volatility

Avoid:

- crowded speculation without business support
- structurally weak businesses with no durable edge
- capital-intensive balance-sheet stress without a clear repair path

## Evidence Threshold

Each recommended stock should include:

- one price/history reason
- one catalyst or policy reason
- one disclosure or fundamental reason

If a candidate misses one of these, lower confidence or remove it.
